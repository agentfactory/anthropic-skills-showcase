Letter from the CEO

To our shareholders, partners, and team members:

Fiscal year 2024 was a year of decisive execution. We entered the year with three commitments to our investors: accelerate enterprise adoption, reach profitability in our core platform segment, and build the organizational capacity to scale. We delivered on all three.

Total revenue grew 34% year-over-year to $412 million, driven by a 41% increase in Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) within our Enterprise tier. Our platform gross margin expanded by 4 percentage points to 71%, reflecting the operating leverage inherent in our architecture. In Q4 2024 we achieved our first quarter of GAAP profitability — a milestone we committed to shareholders at our 2023 Investor Day.

The market we serve is expanding faster than we forecast. Enterprises are no longer evaluating whether to invest in data infrastructure — they are competing on the speed and accuracy of the insights they extract from it. Meridian is the platform at the center of that work for 1,847 enterprise customers across 42 countries.

Looking ahead to 2025, our priorities are clear: deepen our AI-native analytics layer, expand our partner ecosystem, and continue our disciplined march toward durable free cash flow generation. We are operating from a position of strength.

Thank you for your continued trust and support.

Priya Nair
Chief Executive Officer, Meridian Analytics

Product Highlights

Meridian Cortex — AI-Native Analytics

Launched in Q2 2024, Cortex is our AI-native query layer that allows analysts to ask questions in plain language and receive SQL-backed answers with confidence scores. In its first two quarters of availability, Cortex was adopted by 634 enterprise accounts. Customer surveys indicate a 40% reduction in time-to-insight for routine analysis tasks.

Data Mesh Connector Suite

We expanded our connector library from 180 to 247 native integrations in 2024. Key additions include Salesforce Data Cloud, Databricks Unity Catalog, Snowflake Horizon, and direct connectors to all major hyperscaler object storage services. Connector adoption drives platform stickiness: accounts with 10 or more active connectors have 97% annual retention.

Meridian Collaborate

Launched in Q4 2024, Collaborate adds real-time shared workspaces, comment threads on queries, and a version history for dashboards. Early data shows a 22% increase in weekly active users per account in the 30 days post-adoption.

Customer Stories

Vantage Financial Group

Vantage, a global asset manager with $340B AUM, deployed Meridian across its risk analytics and compliance reporting functions. Using Cortex, their compliance team reduced monthly reporting preparation from 14 days to 3 days. "Meridian is now core infrastructure for how we run risk at Vantage," said their Chief Data Officer.

Helix Health Systems

Helix, a regional health system with 18 hospitals, uses Meridian to unify clinical, operational, and financial data across facilities. In 2024 they expanded from a single-region pilot to system-wide deployment. Helix credits Meridian with enabling a 12% reduction in supply chain cost through cross-facility purchasing optimization.

Arcata Logistics

Arcata, one of North America's largest third-party logistics providers, integrated Meridian with their existing ERP and IoT fleet telemetry. Real-time dashboards built on Meridian now surface route optimization recommendations that have saved Arcata an estimated $4.2M in fuel costs annually.

Selected Risk Factors

The following is an abbreviated summary of material risks. A complete discussion of risk factors is available in our Form 10-K filed with the SEC.

  • Macroeconomic conditions affecting enterprise technology spend
  • Competition from large-platform vendors including Microsoft, Google, and Salesforce who bundle analytics capabilities
  • Data privacy and security regulations across jurisdictions, including GDPR, CCPA, and emerging AI governance frameworks
  • Dependence on hyperscaler infrastructure (AWS, Azure, GCP) for platform delivery
  • Ability to attract and retain senior engineering talent in a competitive labor market
  • Customer concentration risk: top 10 customers represent 18% of ARR

Corporate Responsibility

Environmental

Meridian reduced its Scope 1 and 2 carbon emissions by 18% in 2024 versus 2023 baseline, primarily through migration of compute workloads to renewable-energy-powered cloud regions. We are targeting carbon neutrality in Scope 1 and 2 by end of 2026.

Diversity & Inclusion

As of December 31, 2024: 44% of global employees identify as women; 38% of senior leadership roles are held by individuals from underrepresented groups; pay equity audit completed in Q3 2024 confirmed less than 0.5% unexplained pay gap across all roles.

Community

Through the Meridian Foundation, we contributed $2.1M in grants to data literacy programs in 2024, reaching 14,000 students in under-resourced schools across Texas and California.

Notes

  1. All revenue figures are presented in accordance with ASC 606. Comparative periods have been restated to reflect the reclassification of Marketplace revenue from the Professional Services segment, effective Q1 2024.
  2. Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) is a non-GAAP metric defined as the annualized value of all active subscription contracts as of the last day of the reporting period. ARR excludes professional services and one-time license arrangements.
  3. Net Revenue Retention (NRR) is calculated as: (beginning-of-period ARR + expansion ARR — contraction ARR — churned ARR) / beginning-of-period ARR, measured over a trailing twelve-month period.
