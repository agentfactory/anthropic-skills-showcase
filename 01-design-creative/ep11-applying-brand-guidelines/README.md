## Use Case Summary

Take 3 pieces of off-brand copy — a blog intro (too formal), a social post (too hype-y), and a product page headline (generic) — written by different team members and rewrite all of them in one pass to match an established brand voice guide. The fictional brand is **Verdant**, a DTC wellness company with a warm, direct, never-preachy tone.

The "aha moment" hits when all three pieces come back unified: same vocabulary rules, same punctuation style, same personality — even though each started from a completely different register. The iteration step tightens the social post's opening line further and applies the style to a fourth asset (an email subject line) to prove the skill generalises.
