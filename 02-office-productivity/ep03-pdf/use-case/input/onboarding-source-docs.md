## Document 1: Client Services Agreement

**File:** `contracts/client-services-agreement.pdf`  
**Pages:** 14  
**Document Type:** Legal contract  
**Internal page numbering:** 1–14

### Content Summary

This is Meridian Creative's standard client services agreement. The document opens with a recitals section establishing the parties — Meridian Creative LLC (the "Agency") and Apex Digital Inc. (the "Client") — and the effective date of October 15, 2024.

Key sections include:

- **Section 1 — Scope of Services:** References the Statement of Work attached as Exhibit A (the SOW in the package). Notes that additional SOWs may be added by mutual written agreement.
- **Section 2 — Fees and Payment:** Net-30 payment terms. 50% deposit required before project kickoff. Late payments accrue interest at 1.5% per month. Outlines the retainer structure for ongoing work after the initial project.
- **Section 3 — Intellectual Property:** Work product ownership transfers to Client upon receipt of final payment. Agency retains the right to display work in portfolio. Client warrants that any materials provided to Agency are owned or licensed by Client.
- **Section 4 — Confidentiality:** Mutual NDA clause covering project details, pricing, and proprietary information. Survives termination for three years.
- **Section 5 — Revisions and Approval:** Defines what constitutes a revision (changes to approved direction) vs. a refinement (adjustments within approved direction). Includes the revision allotment for each project phase.
- **Section 6 — Termination:** Either party may terminate with 30 days written notice. Client owes payment for work completed through termination date. Kill fee of 25% of remaining contract value applies if client terminates after kickoff.
- **Section 7 — Limitation of Liability:** Agency's liability capped at total fees paid in the preceding 12-month period.
- **Section 8 — General Provisions:** Governing law (Delaware), dispute resolution (mediation before arbitration), entire agreement clause, no waiver language.

**Signature block on page 14.** Two signature lines: one for the Agency (account director) and one for the Client (authorized signatory).

## Document 2: Statement of Work

**File:** `projects/apex-digital-sow.pdf`  
**Pages:** 22  
**Document Type:** Project scope and timeline  
**Internal page numbering:** 1–22

### Content Summary

This Statement of Work covers the Apex Digital Brand Refresh project. It references the Client Services Agreement as the governing contract.

Key sections include:

- **Project Overview (pages 1–2):** Apex Digital is a Series B SaaS company in the workforce management space. Their current brand identity was designed in 2019 and no longer reflects their market position or product maturity. The refresh scope covers visual identity, messaging architecture, and a new website.
- **Phase 1 — Discovery and Strategy (pages 3–5):** Stakeholder interviews (6 participants), competitive landscape analysis, brand audit, customer persona development. Deliverables: brand audit report, strategy brief, positioning statement. Timeline: weeks 1–3. Start date: November 15, 2024.
- **Phase 2 — Visual Identity Development (pages 6–10):** Logo system (primary, secondary, monochrome variants), color palette, typography system, iconography style, photography art direction guidelines. Three initial concepts presented, one selected for refinement. Deliverables: brand identity system files (AI, EPS, PNG, SVG), brand standards document. Timeline: weeks 4–9.
- **Phase 3 — Messaging and Copy (pages 11–13):** Company narrative, tagline options (three presented), website copy (homepage, about, product pages), email templates. Timeline: weeks 5–8 (parallel with Phase 2).
- **Phase 4 — Website Design and Build (pages 14–18):** Information architecture, wireframes, visual design (desktop and mobile), front-end development on Webflow. Includes CMS setup and content migration of existing blog posts. Timeline: weeks 8–16. Note: client is responsible for content approvals within 5 business days of submission.
- **Phase 5 — Launch and Handover (pages 19–21):** QA testing, client training session, DNS migration, post-launch monitoring for 30 days. Deliverables: live website, training recording, asset library handover. Timeline: weeks 16–18.
- **Budget Summary (page 22):** Total project investment: $187,500. Broken down by phase. Payment schedule tied to phase milestones. Excludes third-party costs (stock photography, font licenses, hosting).

## Document 3: Welcome Letter

**File:** `templates/welcome-letter-apex.pdf`  
**Pages:** 2  
**Document Type:** Personal client communication  
**Internal page numbering:** None (letter format)

### Content Summary

This is a two-page letter from the Meridian Creative account director, addressed personally to the Apex Digital CEO. It is warm and specific — not a template boilerplate.

**Page 1:**

The letter opens by expressing genuine enthusiasm for the Apex Digital project. It references a specific moment from the initial discovery call — when the CEO mentioned that their current brand "looks like they're still in beta" — and uses that to frame the ambition of the refresh project. The letter outlines what happens next: the kickoff call is scheduled for October 22nd at 2pm EST, the discovery interviews will begin the week of October 28th, and the Phase 1 deliverables will be in the client's hands by November 8th.

The letter introduces the project team: the account director, the lead strategist, the creative director, and the lead developer. It includes each person's direct email and a note about what they'll be responsible for. This is positioned as "your team, not just a vendor."

**Page 2:**

The second page covers practical matters: how to submit feedback (via the project management tool, not email), response time commitments (Agency responds within one business day), and what to do if something feels off. It explicitly encourages the client to raise concerns early rather than waiting for a formal review checkpoint.

The letter closes with a handwritten-style signature from the account director and a Meridian Creative logo in the footer.
