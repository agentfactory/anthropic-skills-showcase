# Customer Case Study: Apex Digital
## How Apex Digital Recovered 10.5 Hours Per Week and $240K in Year One

**Customer:** Apex Digital Inc.  
**Industry:** Workforce Management SaaS  
**Company Size:** 340 employees, 3 countries (US, Canada, UK)  
**Deployment:** October 2023  
**Review Period:** 12 months post-deployment

---

## Background

Apex Digital is a Series B software company that builds workforce scheduling tools for distributed teams in the logistics and retail sectors. By late 2022, the irony was not lost on anyone internally: a company whose product helps customers manage their workforce was struggling to manage its own.

The problem was systems fragmentation. Over four years of growth, Apex Digital had accumulated three separate HR and scheduling platforms: BambooHR for employee records, a custom-built Airtable setup for shift scheduling, and ADP for payroll. None of these systems talked to each other natively. That gap lived in a set of weekly spreadsheets that two members of the People Operations team maintained manually.

"Every Monday morning, we were doing a three-way reconciliation," said Priya Nair, VP of People Operations at Apex Digital. "Did the hours in Airtable match what was in BambooHR? Did BambooHR match what went to ADP? The margin for error was enormous, and we found that error every time — usually on pay day."

---

## The Challenge

Before deployment, Apex Digital's HR operations team was dealing with three compounding problems:

**Scheduling chaos at scale.** With 340 employees across three time zones and two shift-based operations roles in the UK office, scheduling conflicts averaged 22 per month. Each conflict required manual coordination between a team lead, the employee, and someone in People Ops. Average resolution time: 38 minutes per conflict.

**Payroll reconciliation overhead.** The weekly data reconciliation between the three systems consumed approximately 14 hours of HR staff time per week — the equivalent of one full-time position dedicated entirely to moving data between systems that should have been connected.

**Error rate and its consequences.** The manual reconciliation process produced a payroll error rate of 4.2%. For a company with 340 employees, that meant roughly 14 incorrect paychecks per cycle — corrections that required additional processing time, employee communication, and in some cases, manual bank transfers outside the normal payroll cycle.

---

## The Solution

Apex Digital deployed the unified workforce management platform in October 2023. The implementation covered three phases over six weeks:

- **Phase 1 (weeks 1–2):** Data migration from all three existing systems into the unified platform. Historical records preserved. No employee-facing disruption.
- **Phase 2 (weeks 3–4):** Automated sync rules configured between scheduling, HR records, and payroll. Conflict detection rules set for scheduling overlaps, overtime thresholds, and cross-location coverage gaps.
- **Phase 3 (weeks 5–6):** People Ops team training, manager-level dashboard rollout, and parallel-run period where both the old manual process and the new system ran simultaneously to verify accuracy.

The parallel-run period caught two edge cases in the sync rules that were corrected before full cutover. No payroll errors occurred during or after the transition.

---

## Results (12 Months Post-Deployment)

**Scheduling conflict resolution time:**
- Before: 14 hours/week across 22 conflicts/month (avg 38 min per conflict)
- After: 3.5 hours/week across 8 conflicts/month (avg 26 min per conflict)
- Reduction: 75% in total time spent on scheduling conflicts

**Payroll error rate:**
- Before: 4.2% (approx. 14 errors per payroll cycle)
- After: 0.3% (approx. 1 error per payroll cycle — typically a manual override edge case)
- Reduction: 93% in payroll errors

**Manual reconciliation overhead:**
- Before: 14 hours/week dedicated HR staff time
- After: Eliminated as a discrete task; exception handling only (~1.5 hrs/week)
- Reduction: 89% reduction in reconciliation overhead

**Financial return:**
- Recovered staff productivity (reconciliation time eliminated): ~$94,000/year
- Reduced payroll correction overhead: ~$38,000/year
- Reduced compliance risk exposure: estimated $108,000/year (based on prior-year audit costs)
- Total year-one recovered value: $240,000
- Annual contract value: $42,000
- Net benefit: $198,000
- ROI: 5.7x
- Payback period: 2.1 months
