## Conference Theme

Signal 2025 is about the moment AI moves from research curiosity to mission-critical infrastructure. The theme is **signal vs. noise** — how engineers separate what matters from what doesn't when building systems that can't fail.

The visual language should reflect this: precision, clarity, no decoration for decoration's sake. Every element on the poster should earn its place.

---

## Color Palette Preference

The Signal brand uses a three-color system:
- **Deep Navy:** `#0B1120` — primary background
- **Electric Blue:** `#2D7FEA` — primary accent, links, highlights
- **White:** `#FFFFFF` — primary type, clean negative space

Secondary options (use sparingly):
- **Slate:** `#4A5568` — secondary type, dividers
- **Off-White:** `#F7F8FA` — large text backgrounds if needed

Do not introduce additional colors. Brand purity matters.

## Specific Ask

Design a **bold typographic poster** that can stand alone at the venue entrance and in the conference program.

Requirements:
- All four keynote speaker names must be clearly legible
- Speaker titles and companies may appear at smaller size
- Event name "SIGNAL 2025" should dominate the composition
- Tagline "The Future of AI Infrastructure" must appear
- Date (November 14–15, 2025) and venue (Yerba Buena Center for the Arts, San Francisco) must appear
- Website URL (`signal2025.ai`) must appear

Creative direction:
- **Style:** Bold typographic — let the type do the work, not decoration
- **Tone:** Must feel **premium, not corporate**. This is a serious technical conference, not a vendor expo. No stock photo textures, no gradient blobs, no "AI aesthetic" clichés.
- **Inspiration:** Think editorial — WSJ Magazine, Wired feature spreads, architectural announcements. The kind of poster someone would actually want on their wall.
- **Typography:** Condensed or display weights for headlines. Clean sans-serif for details. High contrast between text sizes.
- **Hierarchy:** Event name first, then speakers, then logistics. The viewer should read in that order without being told to.

---

## What to Avoid

- Generic "tech conference" aesthetics: floating circuit board lines, hexagons, particle effects
- Clip art or placeholder icons
- More than three font weights in active use
- Centered alignment for everything (boring and reads as corporate)
- Gradients as a substitute for design decisions
- Anything that would look at home on a pharmaceutical company's annual report

The primary deliverable is the HTML/CSS file ready for PDF export. No layered source files required for this brief.
