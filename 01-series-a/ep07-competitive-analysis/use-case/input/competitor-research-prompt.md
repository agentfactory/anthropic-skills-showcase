# Competitive Research Brief — Flowdesk

## Company Context

**Company:** Flowdesk
**Product:** Async-first project management platform for distributed remote teams
**Stage:** Seed (18 months post-launch, 200 paying customers)
**Pricing:** $29/seat/month (one tier, no free plan, 14-day trial)
**Core value proposition:** Project management designed for teams that don't work the same hours — async by default, not as an afterthought
**Primary market:** Remote-first companies, 10–75 employees, knowledge-work focused (product, engineering, design, marketing)
**Key differentiator (our belief):** We build around written, timestamped context — every update, decision, and handoff is documented automatically, not optionally

---

## Competitors to Analyze

Please analyze the following three competitors in depth. For each, follow a consistent schema (defined below).

### Competitor 1: Asana
- URL: asana.com
- Known for: Enterprise workflow management, cross-functional project visibility, timeline/Gantt views
- Relevant tiers: Starter ($13.49/seat/mo), Advanced ($30.49/seat/mo), Enterprise (custom)

### Competitor 2: Monday.com
- URL: monday.com
- Known for: Visual project management, high customizability, non-technical team adoption
- Relevant tiers: Basic ($12/seat/mo), Standard ($14/seat/mo), Pro ($24/seat/mo), Enterprise (custom)

### Competitor 3: Linear
- URL: linear.app
- Known for: Developer-first issue tracking, speed, opinionated workflow for engineering teams
- Relevant tiers: Free (up to 250 issues), Plus ($8/seat/mo), Business ($16/seat/mo)

---

## Output Schema (use for each competitor)

For each competitor, provide:

1. **Positioning statement** — How do they describe themselves? What's the core claim?
2. **Price per seat (comparable tier)** — Most relevant tier for a 20–50 person team
3. **Core ICP** — Who is this product actually built for?
4. **Top 3 strengths** — What do they do genuinely well?
5. **Key weakness** — What's the most honest, specific limitation (not a generic "too expensive")?
6. **Differentiation gap for Flowdesk** — What does this competitor not do well that Flowdesk could credibly own?

---

## Specific Questions to Answer

After the per-competitor sections, please address these questions:

1. **Positioning gap:** Is there a viable "async-native" positioning lane that none of these three competitors currently own? What's the evidence for or against?

2. **Pricing comparison:** At 30 seats, what does each competitor cost monthly? Where does Flowdesk's $29/seat sit relative to the field — is it premium, mid-market, or budget-adjacent?

3. **Feature whitespace:** Based on public information, what feature category is weakest across all three competitors that a small remote team would care about?

4. **ICP overlap:** Which competitor's customer base overlaps most directly with Flowdesk's target? Who is Flowdesk most likely to displace in a head-to-head evaluation?

5. **Sales handles:** For each competitor, give one objection a prospect might raise ("we already use Asana") and the strongest honest counter Flowdesk could use.

---

## Format Instructions

- Use markdown with clear headings and subheadings
- Use a comparison table at the end that summarizes all six schema fields across all three competitors
- Keep each competitor section to 300–400 words
- The comparison table should highlight the "Differentiation Gap" row prominently — this is the most actionable output
- Be specific and honest — avoid generic observations; favor insights that are directly useful for sales conversations
