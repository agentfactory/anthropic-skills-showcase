# Luma — Copy Samples for Brand Voice Analysis

**Company:** Luma
**Category:** Expense management software for remote and hybrid teams
**Stage:** Series A — approximately 18 months post-launch, 400 customers, growing into enterprise

These five samples are used as input for the `brand-voice` skill demo. Each represents a different content type and channel, giving the skill enough signal to detect the underlying voice system.

---

## Sample 1 — Website Hero Headline

**Context:** Above-the-fold hero section on the luma.so homepage. Paired with a subheadline and a single CTA button.

> **Expenses that don't make you groan.**
>
> Luma connects to every card your team uses, categorizes spend automatically, and closes the books without a single email chain. Remote teams run cleaner here.

---

## Sample 2 — Email Subject Line

**Context:** Cold outreach to finance leads at Series B startups. Sent from a named sales rep, not a marketing alias.

> Re: the $47k in untagged travel spend sitting in your Q2 report

---

## Sample 3 — Tweet

**Context:** @LumaHQ Twitter/X account. Posted on a Tuesday at 10am. No image, no thread — just the text.

> Expense reports are a form of punishment invented by people who hated their finance team.
>
> We fixed that. Quietly.

---

## Sample 4 — Product Feature Description

**Context:** In-app tooltip that appears the first time a user opens the Reconciliation view. Character limit: 180 words.

> **Smart Reconciliation**
>
> Luma matches every transaction to a receipt, a category, and a policy rule before you ever open this page. If something doesn't match, it surfaces here — flagged, explained, and ready for a one-click decision.
>
> No digging through spreadsheets. No pinging people on Slack. The work is already done; this view is just where you confirm it.

---

## Sample 5 — 404 Error Message

**Context:** Custom 404 page displayed when a user hits a broken or expired link. Includes a button labeled "Back to dashboard."

> **This page went on an offsite.**
>
> Honestly, we didn't approve that trip. The link you followed doesn't exist anymore — or maybe it never did. Either way, your dashboard is exactly where you left it.
